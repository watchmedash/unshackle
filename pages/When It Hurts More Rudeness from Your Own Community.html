When It Hurts More: Rudeness from Your Own Community



So I’ve been living as an expat for quite some time, and I’ve also spent a good number of years in Western countries. I’m Pakistani myself, but I’ve noticed something odd — sometimes it feels like other Pakistanis here are unusually rude for no reason.

Today I went to a bank in Sharjah, and the staff member (also Pakistani, I believe) looked at me with a disgusted expression when I asked a question. His tone and body language felt unnecessarily dismissive. I didn’t say or do anything odd — just a normal banking query.

Maybe I’m overthinking it, but has anyone else experienced something similar?

When it’s someone from your own community, it hits differently. It feels heavier. There’s a strange kind of disappointment when the person on the other end of the coldness shares your background, your culture, even your language.

I’ve felt more welcome and respected in places where I was a complete outsider than in moments like today.

I’m not writing this to generalize or call anyone out — I know there are kind and respectful people everywhere. But sometimes, the silence around these interactions makes them feel even more isolating.

All I’m saying is — kindness costs nothing. And when it comes from your own people, it means everything.
