I’m Pregnant — And He Blew Smoke in My Face



This morning, while walking to my office, something happened that left me completely shattered.

I’m pregnant. It’s early in the morning. I’m just trying to start my day like everyone else, walking peacefully to work.

Then, out of nowhere — a stranger lights his cigarette, looks straight at me, and deliberately blows the smoke right into my face. He knew what he was doing. And then… he laughed. He laughed and walked away, like I was nothing.

I stood there for a moment, stunned. Sad. Hurt. Angry. Not just because of the smoke, but because of the meanness. The complete disregard for my wellbeing — and for my baby.

What did I do to deserve that?

I kept asking myself that question all day. I said nothing to him. I wasn’t in his way. I didn’t look at him. But somehow, that man decided I was worth mocking. Worth disrespecting.

People talk about kindness, about community, about the importance of supporting women — especially during pregnancy. But moments like this remind me that some hearts are cold. That some people carry cruelty for no reason.

I don’t want pity. I just want decency. I want to feel safe when I walk outside. I want to believe that being pregnant won’t make me a target for mockery or harm.

To anyone reading this: please, think before you act. A little respect — a little empathy — can mean everything to someone who’s already carrying so much.
