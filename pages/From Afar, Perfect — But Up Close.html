From Afar, Perfect — But Up Close?

Note: The image is AI-generated and may not accurately reflect the people or setting in the story.


There’s a gentleman in our office — the kind of person who turns heads.

Always sharply dressed. Confident. Handsome. From a distance, he’s the kind of guy many would look up to or want to emulate. He fits the image of success.

But then you get a little closer… and things change.

If he’s been in the office alone, the air carries a strong body odour. When he speaks, the smell from his breath makes it difficult to stand near him. It’s awkward. Uncomfortable. And honestly, it’s something no one wants to address.

Because of this, I find myself avoiding close interaction. I prefer messaging him on Teams or talking from a distance, just to dodge the awkwardness.

But here’s the question — should someone tell him?

Is it more respectful to stay silent and keep avoiding, or to quietly let him know so he can do something about it?

Body odour and breath issues can be deeply personal and often linked to health or stress — sometimes the person genuinely has no idea. But it’s a sensitive topic. No one wants to feel embarrassed or shamed.

So what’s the right move?

Personally, I’m still unsure. But I do know this — kindness and discretion go a long way. If I ever muster the courage, it won’t be to insult. It’ll be to help.

And maybe that’s what we all need more of in the workplace — not just honesty, but the courage to be kind when it's uncomfortable.
